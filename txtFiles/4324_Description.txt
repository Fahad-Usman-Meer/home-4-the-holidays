&nbsp;
<p class="MsoNormal" style="TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><span style="FONT-SIZE: 11pt">The Cat Shack, Inc. is an all volunteer, non-profit no-kill cat rescue organization, incorporated in the state of <st1:state w:st="on"><st1:place w:st="on">Pennsylvania</st1:place></st1:state> in July, 2001.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes">&nbsp; </span>The group does not have a shelter and relies on volunteers to foster cats in their homes.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes">&nbsp; </span>There are approximately 25 volunteers currently working for the organization.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes">&nbsp; </span>The reliance on foster homes, the no-kill policy and limited financial resources place&nbsp;constraints on the number of cats that are brought into the rescue.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes">&nbsp; </span>The rescue is recognized by the IRS as a 501(c)(3) organization and thus all donations made by the general public are tax deductible.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes">&nbsp;&nbsp; </span></span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal" style="TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><span style="FONT-SIZE: 11pt"><span style="mso-spacerun: yes"></span>The mission of the organization is:<o:p></o:p></span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal" style="TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><span style="FONT-SIZE: 11pt"><o:p>&nbsp;</o:p></span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal" style="TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><strong style="mso-bidi-font-weight: normal"><span style="FONT-SIZE: 11pt">&ldquo;To help reduce the unnecessary euthanasia of adoptable cats, to reduce the overpopulation of cats, to reduce the cruelty to and neglect of cats and to educate the general public in cat related issues.&rdquo;<span style="mso-spacerun: yes">&nbsp; </span><o:p></o:p></span></strong></p>
<p class="MsoNormal" style="TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><span style="FONT-SIZE: 11pt"><o:p>&nbsp;</o:p></span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal" style="TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><span style="FONT-SIZE: 11pt">To achieve this mission, the organization is involved in four main activities.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes">&nbsp; </span>These are: <span style="mso-spacerun: yes">&nbsp;</span>(estimated percentage of time spent on each is listed in parenthesis): <o:p></o:p></span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal" style="TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><span style="FONT-SIZE: 11pt"><o:p>&nbsp;</o:p></span></p>
<ol style="MARGIN-TOP: 0in" type="1">
    <li class="MsoNormal" style="TEXT-ALIGN: justify; mso-list: l0 level1 lfo1; tab-stops: list .5in"><strong style="mso-bidi-font-weight: normal"><em style="mso-bidi-font-style: normal"><span style="FONT-SIZE: 11pt">Adoption of stray or unwanted cats and kittens into house and barn homes (60%)</span></em></strong><span style="FONT-SIZE: 11pt"> &ndash; All cats adopted out from the Cat Shack are spayed or neutered, inoculated for rabies and distemper, wormed and treated for fleas and ticks and tested for both Feline Leukemia (FeLV) and Feline Aids (FIV).<span style="mso-spacerun: yes">&nbsp; </span>Cats that are designated as &ldquo;barn&rdquo; cats (wild or feral cats that need to be placed in outside, rural homes) are also inoculated for Feline Leukemia.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes">&nbsp; </span>Any other required medical attention is also given.<span style="mso-tab-count: 11">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; </span><span style="mso-tab-count: 1">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; </span><o:p></o:p></span></li>
    <li class="MsoNormal" style="TEXT-ALIGN: justify; mso-list: l0 level1 lfo1; tab-stops: list .5in"><strong style="mso-bidi-font-weight: normal"><em style="mso-bidi-font-style: normal"><span style="FONT-SIZE: 11pt">Trap, Neuter, Release Programs for Feral Cat Colonies (15%) </span></em></strong><span style="FONT-SIZE: 11pt">- In order to help control the expanding cat population, a Trap, Neuter, Release (TNR) program has been implemented.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes">&nbsp; </span>Cats in this program are usually feral (wild) cats that are unlikely to ever become house pets.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes">&nbsp; </span>Many of these cats have gathered in fairly large colonies in locations where food, water and shelter are available.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes">&nbsp; </span>Many times a colony begins with the good intentions of a concerned person who finds a stray cat at their door but soon loses control of the situation and is feeding a large number of strays that continue to reproduce.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes">&nbsp; </span>While The Cat Shack will make every effort to find good barn homes for most feral cats, some of the colonies that have not grown too large can benefit from TNR.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes">&nbsp; </span>These cats will be trapped, given a general health assessment, including any necessary treatments and inoculations, spayed or neutered and then returned to the colony.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes">&nbsp; </span>The goal is to drastically reduce or eliminate the rate of reproduction in these situations.<span style="mso-tab-count: 1">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; </span><span style="mso-tab-count: 5">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; </span><o:p></o:p></span></li>
    <li class="MsoNormal" style="TEXT-ALIGN: justify; mso-list: l0 level1 lfo1; tab-stops: list .5in"><strong style="mso-bidi-font-weight: normal"><em style="mso-bidi-font-style: normal"><span style="FONT-SIZE: 11pt">Low Cost Spay and Neuter Clinics (15%)</span></em></strong><span style="FONT-SIZE: 11pt"> - The Cat Shack uses the services of licensed veterinarians to spay and neuter cats in the Cat Shack organization, cats in other lrescue organizations, cats in the TRN program and cats residing in low income homes whose owners can not afford the normal cost of a spay/neuter operation.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes">&nbsp; </span>These clinics are not affiliated with any veterinary office and are conducted in locations determined by the Cat Shack.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes">&nbsp; </span>The Cat Shack purchases all equipment, drugs and vaccinations for the clinics.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes">&nbsp; </span>Vets are paid on a per cat basis.<span style="mso-tab-count: 1">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; </span><o:p></o:p></span></li>
    <li class="MsoNormal" style="TEXT-ALIGN: justify; mso-list: l0 level1 lfo1; tab-stops: list .5in"><strong style="mso-bidi-font-weight: normal"><em style="mso-bidi-font-style: normal"><span style="FONT-SIZE: 11pt">Education of the Public in General Cat Related Issues (10%)</span></em></strong><span style="FONT-SIZE: 11pt"> - The Cat Shack educates the public on various cat related topics and issues.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes">&nbsp; </span>These include: the benefits of spaying and neutering their pets, alternatives to declawing a cat, disease prevention and education of other health related issues and the feral population problem and how to try to control it.<o:p></o:p></span></li>
</ol>
